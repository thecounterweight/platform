## CONTRIBUTION AGREEMENT

**Date:** [DATE]

**Contributor:** [CONTRIBUTOR FULL NAME]

### 1. Contribution

The Contributor transfers **[AMOUNT] [CURRENCY]** to the Recipient on [DATE] via [METHOD — UPI/bank transfer/etc.], toward the development and operation of The Counterweight platform (https://thecounterweight.org).

### 2. Nature of Contribution

This is risk capital. It is not a loan, gift, donation, or equity purchase.

- There is no repayment obligation.
- There is no interest or guaranteed return.
- There is no maturity date or repayment schedule.
- No ownership of any kind is transferred.

The Contributor makes this contribution with full knowledge that it may be lost entirely if the project does not generate revenue.

### 3. Conditional Return

If The Counterweight platform generates revenue, the Contributor is entitled to returns under the platform's revenue-share framework. Specific terms (percentage, duration, cap) will be decided by the democratic governance process described in the platform's funding model, within the constitutional bounds below.

### 4. Constitutional Bounds

Regardless of how governance decides specific terms:

- Investor share will never exceed 30% of total platform revenue under $20M, or 40% at $20M+
- All revenue-share agreements are time-bounded
- Returns are proportional to contribution amount
- Earlier contributions receive a time-held advantage over later contributions

### 5. Entity Formation

When The Counterweight's operating entity is formally incorporated, the entity recognizes this contribution under its revenue-share framework. The Contributor's record, amount, and timestamp are preserved exactly as recorded.

### 6. Record

This contribution is recorded in the public ledger repository (`thecounterweight/ledger`), GPG-signed, with the full history archived at archive.org and Software Heritage. The Contributor may choose to be identified by full name or pseudonym in the public record.

### 7. Use of Funds

Contributions are used for platform development and operations — including but not limited to infrastructure, development tools, verification services, and operational expenses. All spending is recorded publicly in the same ledger.

### 8. Risk Acknowledgment

The Contributor acknowledges:

- This is risk capital that may be lost entirely
- No legal entity currently exists for The Counterweight
- The Recipient has no personal repayment obligation if the project fails
- Returns are conditional on the platform generating revenue
- Specific revenue-share terms have not been finalized
- This contribution should be an amount the Contributor can afford to lose

### 9. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of [JURISDICTION — typically the Contributor's country of residence].
